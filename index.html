SCOTUS has recently decided to overturn Roe v. Wade, allowing states to take away the right to an abortion. In Delaware, abortions are still safe and legal, but others are not as lucky. How to Help

In Pennsylvania, abortion is not legal after 23 weeks and 6 days unless the pregnant person’s life or physical health is at serious risk. Otherwise, one would have to travel outside of Pennslyvania to receive abortion care, which is completely legal. To help those in Pennsylvania seeking abortion care, you can donate to the Abortion Liberation Fund of PA here, which helps patients statewide, or any other organizations listed below:

Western Pennsylvania Fund for Choice

Allentown Women's Center

The Women’s Center (Delaware County and Philadelphia)

Planned Parenthood (Southeastern Pennsylvania, Western Pennsylvania, and Keystone)

On the other hand, abortion in Maryland is legal until viability (24-26 weeks) except for cases where the pregnant person’s life, general health (physical and mental), or the fetus’s health is at risk. In other cases, someone would have to travel outside of Maryland to receive an abortion which, yet again, is legal. To help those in Maryland seeking abortion care, you can donate to the Baltimore Abortion Fund here, which helps people state wide, including their DuPont clinic in D.C., or donate to any of the other organizations listed below:

Roe v. Wade Timeline

Roe v. Wade

The Supreme Court hears the case of Norma McCorvey, under the alias Jane Roe, against Henry Wade, the district attorney of Dallas County at the time. Wade had enforced a law which prohibited abortion in Texas, except to save a woman’s life.

1971

The Decision

The SCOTUS, in a 7-2 decision, rules that women have the right to an abortion under the Fourteenth amendment to the Constitution.

1973

Conservative Majority

Donald Trump appoints three conservative judges to the Supreme Court in his tenure as President of the United States: Neil Gorsuch, Brett Kavanaugh, and Amy Coney Barrett. These choices solidify the SCOTUS’s conservative majority.

2016

Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization

Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization is brought to the Supreme Court, dealing with a Mississippi Law that bans abortion after 15 weeks.

Upholding this law would lead to the overturning of Roe v. Wade.

2021

The Overturn

The SCOTUS rules 6-4 in favor of upholding the Mississippi Law, and 5-4 in favor of overturning Roe v. Wade. The decision leads to giving the states the power to ban abortion.
